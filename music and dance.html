Traditional Basotho music and dances

Basotho are people filled with love and their cultural heritage is the most thing they value,they showcase it via traditional dances and music.This bring unity and storytelling among the community and it does not only end there.This things also apply in ceremonies.Below is the descriptions of Basotho traditional dances and music.

Traditional Basotho dances

  1. Mohobelo: A high-energy stomping dance traditionally performed by men. It involves synchronized movements and high kicks that demonstrate endurance and energy.



  2. Mokhibo: A traditional women's dance performed while kneeling. The dancers move their upper bodies and sweep their hands in the air, creating a gentle rising and falling motion in rhythm with the music.

  3. Mokorotlo: A male dance performed on important occasions and rituals, such as when appearing before a chief. Historically, it was also performed before battle to instill courage and motivation.

  4. Focho: A dance performed with stomping and shoulder movements, often initiated by a skilled lesiba player. It is a group dance where participants follow the leader's specific movements.

  5. Litolobonya: A dance performed by women to celebrate a new mother and child, often at a small, all-female party called a pitiki.

  6. Gumboot dance: A rhythmic stomping dance performed by male migrant laborers, originating from the South African mines. The dance, originally a form of communication, uses synchronized slapping of gumboots and stamping.

  7. Mangae: A dance style associated with initiation schools (lebollo) and the popular famo music genre. Initiates sing and dance mangae upon returning from the mountains.

  8. Pina-tsa-ts'iloana: A rhythmic movement performed by women while grinding corn or millet. It is not a formal dance, but a rhythmic work activity accompanied by song.

  9. Lits'omo: A type of musical storytelling, where stories are often accompanied by music and rhythmic actions. It serves as a tool for children's development and education.

  10. Lihlahla: A type of sung poetry that serves as an expressive form of Basotho music and dance.

  11. Lifela: Songs and hymns, which can be part of dances and ceremonies. They can cover themes from traveling and love to initiation rites.

Traditional Basotho instruments



  1. Lesiba: Lesotho's national musical instrument, often called the "herdboy's gramophone". It is a "stringed-wind" instrument with a string stretched over a piece of hardwood. The player produces sound by blowing and sucking air across a flattened quill attached to the string.

  2. Thomo: A stringed, gourd-resonated bow instrument, traditionally played by women. It is played by stopping the strings with a finger while striking it with a stick.

  3. Mamokhorong (or sekhankula): A bowed monochord instrument that resembles a one-stringed violin. It is often made from a five-liter tin can resonator and is traditionally played by herders.

  4. Lekope: A stick-pipe instrument made from a hollowed river reed. It has a string, traditionally made of horsehair, that is plucked and stopped with a finger.

  5. Sekebeku: A wooden jaw harp, often played by girls and women. A Basotho version is sometimes made from bamboo.

  6. Lekolulo: A kind of flute traditionally played by herding boys.

  7. Setolo-tolo: A men's instrument that is played with the mouth and resembles an extended jaw harp. It is now endangered and rarely played by younger generations.

  8. Lekhitlane: A horn, sometimes referred to as a whistle, used by traditional doctors and mediums for spiritual purposes. Due to its sacred nature, representations of it were often considered taboo.

  9. Lenaka: A horn, similar to the lekhitlane, with spiritual significance.

  10. Sekupu: A traditional drum, often used to accompany dances and rituals.

  11. Marothloane (or merithloane): Ankle rattles made from cocoons, seeds, or other natural materials. They are worn by dancers to provide percussion and rhythm.

  12. Moropa: A type of drum, mentioned in historical accounts of Basotho music.
